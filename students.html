Open positions

PhD students

Fully funded · Fall 2026 and beyond

I am looking for ambitious PhD students with a strong background in machine learning, mathematics, computer science, or related areas, eager to commit to multi-year research on trustworthy machine learning.

MSc students

I welcome MSc students interested in trustworthy ML. Funding is not guaranteed but is possible through scholarships, teaching assistantships, and research grants.

Undergraduate researchers

NSERC USRA, internships, capstone

I supervise motivated undergraduates on focused research projects, including NSERC USRA-funded summer projects, the Vector AI Research Internship, capstone projects, and volunteer / for-credit research.

Visiting students

Short-term collaborations

I welcome short-term visiting students from other universities for collaborative research projects on trustworthy ML, typically lasting one term or a summer.

What I look for in a student

  • Curiosity about open problems — willingness to dig into questions where the right answer isn't obvious yet, including ones that fall between conventional sub-fields.
  • Rigorous empirical thinking — care for sound experiments, honest reporting of negative results, and reproducibility.
  • Strong communication — willingness to ask questions, share work in progress, and engage with feedback. Clear writing matters.
  • Initiative and ownership — comfort scoping your own sub-projects within a broader research direction, rather than waiting for week-by-week direction.

A formal CS background is welcome but not required — I supervise students from engineering, mathematics, statistics, and adjacent fields.

How to apply

  1. Fill out the application form. This is the fastest way to reach me — please use it instead of cold email.
  2. If your background looks like a fit, I will reach out to schedule a chat.
  3. For PhD and MSc applicants, you will also need to apply formally through the University of Ottawa graduate admissions portal in parallel.

I do my best to read every application, but I may not be able to reply to all of them individually. Please don't be discouraged if you don't hear back — it doesn't reflect on the strength of your application.
